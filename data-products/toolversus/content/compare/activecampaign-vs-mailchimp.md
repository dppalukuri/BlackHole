## The Quick Answer

**ActiveCampaign** is for businesses that take email seriously — lead nurturing sequences, sales automation, CRM integration, and sophisticated segmentation. **Mailchimp** is for businesses that want something quick and simple to send newsletters and basic campaigns.

If you'll outgrow "send a newsletter every week," start with ActiveCampaign. You'll save the migration headache later.

## Pricing

| Contacts | ActiveCampaign (Starter) | Mailchimp (Standard) |
|----------|------------------------|---------------------|
| 1,000 | $15/mo | $30/mo |
| 5,000 | $79/mo | $75/mo |
| 10,000 | $111/mo | $110/mo |
| 25,000 | $179/mo | $270/mo |

Surprise: **ActiveCampaign is cheaper** at most tiers, especially at 1,000 contacts ($15 vs $30) and 25,000+ contacts. Mailchimp's pricing has increased significantly since the Intuit acquisition.

ActiveCampaign's major disadvantage: **no free plan**. Mailchimp's free tier (500 contacts) lets you start without paying. ActiveCampaign offers a 14-day free trial.

## Automation — The Decisive Difference

This is where ActiveCampaign destroys Mailchimp:

| Capability | ActiveCampaign | Mailchimp |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Visual automation builder | Advanced (if/else, splits, waits, goals) | Basic (linear journeys) |
| Conditional logic | Full if/then/else branching | Limited |
| Lead scoring | Built-in, customizable | Not available |
| CRM | Built-in with deal pipeline | Not available |
| Site tracking | Tracks page visits, triggers automations | Basic |
| Predictive sending | AI optimizes send time per contact | Basic send-time optimization |

If you're doing anything beyond "send email to everyone on Tuesday," ActiveCampaign's automation is worth the switch.

## Where Mailchimp Wins

- **Free plan** — start for $0 with 500 contacts
- **Easier to learn** — simpler interface, less overwhelming for beginners
- **Better email designer** — more templates, easier drag-and-drop
- **Brand recognition** — "We use Mailchimp" is universally understood

## FAQ

<details>
<summary>I'm just starting out with 200 subscribers. Which should I pick?</summary>
<div class="answer">Start with Mailchimp's free plan. At 200 subscribers, you don't need advanced automation yet. When you hit 1,000+ subscribers and want to build proper nurture sequences, evaluate ActiveCampaign. Migrating at that point is straightforward.</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Which has better deliverability?</summary>
<div class="answer">ActiveCampaign consistently ranks in the top tier for email deliverability. Their stricter account policies and smaller user base (compared to Mailchimp's 13M accounts) mean a cleaner sender reputation. Mailchimp's deliverability is good but has been more variable as they've scaled.</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can ActiveCampaign replace my CRM?</summary>
<div class="answer">For small to mid-size businesses, yes. ActiveCampaign's built-in CRM handles contacts, deals, pipeline stages, and task management. It's not as deep as Salesforce or HubSpot, but for businesses under 50 employees, it eliminates the need for a separate CRM tool and the data sync headaches that come with it.</div>
</details>
