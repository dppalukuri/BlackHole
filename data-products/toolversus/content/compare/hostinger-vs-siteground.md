## The Quick Answer

**Hostinger** for most people — comparable speed at less than half the renewal price. **SiteGround** if customer support is your top priority or you're an agency managing multiple client sites.

## Pricing — The Elephant in the Room

| | Hostinger (Premium) | SiteGround (StartUp) |
|--|---------------------|---------------------|
| Intro price | $1.99/mo | $1.99/mo |
| Renewal price | **$7.99/mo** | **$17.99/mo** |
| Websites | 100 | 1 |
| Storage | 100 GB NVMe | 10 GB SSD |

Same introductory price, but SiteGround's renewal is **125% more expensive** and gives you 1 website with 10 GB storage, while Hostinger gives 100 websites with 100 GB. Over 3 years at renewal prices, you'd pay **$360 more** with SiteGround.

## Speed

Both are fast, but for different reasons:
- **Hostinger:** LiteSpeed servers + built-in caching + NVMe storage
- **SiteGround:** Custom SuperCacher + NGINX + proprietary CDN

In benchmarks, they're within ~0.2 seconds of each other. Both achieve sub-1.5 second load times for optimized WordPress sites. Speed should not be your deciding factor.

## Support — Where SiteGround Earns Its Premium

SiteGround's customer support is genuinely best-in-class:
- Average response time under 10 minutes
- Support agents can actually solve technical problems (not just follow scripts)
- WordPress-specific expertise — they'll debug your plugin conflicts

Hostinger's chat support is good but takes longer and is less technical. If you're non-technical and need hand-holding, SiteGround's support is worth the premium.
