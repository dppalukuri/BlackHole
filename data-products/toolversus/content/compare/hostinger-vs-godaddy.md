## The Quick Answer

**Hostinger wins.** GoDaddy is a great domain registrar but a mediocre web host. Hostinger is 3x cheaper, 50% faster, and rated significantly higher by users on every review platform. GoDaddy's only advantages are brand familiarity and 24/7 phone support.

## Pricing

| | Hostinger (Premium) | GoDaddy (Economy) |
|--|---------------------|-------------------|
| Intro price | **$1.99/mo** | $5.99/mo |
| Renewal | ~$7.99/mo | ~$11.99/mo |
| Websites | 100 | 1 |
| Storage | 100 GB NVMe | 25 GB |

Hostinger is **3x cheaper at intro** and 33% cheaper at renewal while offering 100 websites and 4x the storage.

## Speed

- **Hostinger:** LiteSpeed servers + NVMe SSDs → ~1.2s average load time
- **GoDaddy:** Apache servers + standard SSDs → ~2.0-2.5s average load time

GoDaddy's hosting infrastructure hasn't kept pace with modern hosts. They've invested heavily in their website builder and domain business, but the shared hosting product shows its age.

## User Ratings Tell the Story

| Platform | Hostinger | GoDaddy |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| G2 | **4.6/5** | 3.4/5 |
| Trustpilot | **4.7/5** (65K reviews) | 4.6/5 (40K reviews) |

GoDaddy's G2 rating of 3.4 is one of the lowest among major hosts. Common complaints: aggressive upselling, expensive renewals, and underperforming hosting.

## When GoDaddy Makes Sense

- You already have domains on GoDaddy and want everything in one dashboard
- You need 24/7 phone support (Hostinger is chat-only)
- You're non-technical and want the simplest possible all-in-one (domain + hosting + email + builder)
