## Privacy architecture

This is where ProtonVPN earns its premium:

**ProtonVPN:**
- **Switzerland**-based — outside 14-Eyes, strong privacy legislation.
- **Fully open-source apps** on every platform — rare and genuinely significant.
- Independently audited by SEC Consult (2022, 2024).
- **Secure Core** multi-hop routes through hardened Swiss/Iceland/Sweden nodes.
- Made by the team behind Proton Mail — recognised brand in the privacy-tool space.

**NordVPN:**
- VPN operations registered in **Panama** (no data retention laws). Corporate parent **Nord Security** is headquartered in Lithuania; Panama jurisdiction is what governs user-data handling.
- Apps are proprietary (not open source). The **NordLynx** protocol is a proprietary WireGuard-based implementation — unlike the standard WireGuard that ProtonVPN ships, it has not been independently protocol-audited.
- Independently audited no-logs policy by Deloitte (2022, 2023) + PwC (2020).
- Uses **diskless / RAM-only servers** (fully rolled out 2023).
- Nord Security merged with **Surfshark** in 2022 — the two brands now share a corporate parent but operate separate infrastructure, apps, and teams.
- In 2018, an unauthorised third party accessed one of NordVPN's Finnish datacenter servers — no user logs or credentials were exposed (none are kept), and the incident was publicly confirmed by NordVPN in 2019. They've since moved to a colocated-server model with stricter vetting.

Both have strong audited no-logs records; ProtonVPN's fully open-source stack is the more transparent of the two, and uses audited upstream WireGuard rather than a proprietary fork.

**Edge: ProtonVPN** on transparency and protocol openness; **NordVPN** on audit frequency.

## FAQ

**Is ProtonVPN's free plan actually usable?**
Yes — genuinely. Unlimited data, 3 country options (US, NL, JP), one device at a time. Most "free VPN" services are either ad-supported or actively selling your data; ProtonVPN's free plan is genuinely free and privacy-preserving because they treat it as a loss-leader for their paid product.

**Can I use both at the same time?**
Not normally — VPN-over-VPN ("chained VPN") usually breaks both providers' kill switches and halves your speed. If you want multi-hop, use NordVPN's Double VPN or ProtonVPN's Secure Core instead of running two VPNs at once.

**Which works better in the UAE / China?**
Both have obfuscated/stealth servers. NordVPN is slightly more reliable in the UAE based on independent reports. For mainland China, ExpressVPN is still the most reliable; neither NordVPN nor ProtonVPN are guaranteed to work.

**Which is faster for 4K streaming?**
Both are fast enough. On a 100 Mbps connection, both will run 4K without issues. On a gigabit connection, NordVPN slightly edges ProtonVPN, but you're unlikely to notice the difference.

**Is ProtonVPN worth the extra money just for Switzerland?**
Not on its own. Swiss jurisdiction is a real advantage vs Panama's (though both are strong), but it's a small edge that matters only if you're in a specific threat model. For 99% of users, the price + speed + server count wins out.
