## The Quick Answer

Both are excellent platforms loved by creators and small businesses. **MailerLite** wins on pure value — more features, lower price, higher G2 rating (4.7 vs 4.4). **ConvertKit (Kit)** wins if you need built-in digital product sales, the Creator Network for audience growth, or you specifically identify as a content creator.

## Pricing

| Subscribers | ConvertKit (Creator) | MailerLite (Growing Business) |
|-------------|---------------------|------------------------------|
| Free tier | 10,000 subs (limited) | 1,000 subs (12K emails/mo) |
| 1,000 | $25/mo | $15/mo |
| 5,000 | $79/mo | $39/mo |
| 10,000 | $119/mo | $73/mo |

MailerLite is **40-50% cheaper** on paid plans. The free tier comparison is interesting — ConvertKit offers more subscribers (10K vs 1K) but with severe feature limitations (no automation, no sequences). MailerLite's free plan includes automation.

| Feature | ConvertKit | MailerLite |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Sell digital products | Yes (built-in commerce) | No |
| Paid subscriptions | Yes | Yes |
| Creator Network | Yes (cross-recommendations) | No |
| Newsletter referral system | Yes | No |
| Sponsorship marketplace | Yes | No |
| Website builder | Basic | Better |
| Blog | No | Yes (built-in) |

ConvertKit's creator ecosystem (network + commerce + referrals) is genuinely unique. If you monetize through digital products, this alone justifies the price premium.

## Email Design Philosophy

These tools take opposite approaches:

**ConvertKit:** Deliberately minimal, plain-text-focused emails. The philosophy is that simple emails feel more personal and have better deliverability. You can customize, but the defaults push you toward text.

**MailerLite:** Full drag-and-drop designer with rich templates. If you want visually polished newsletters with images, columns, and branded designs, MailerLite is significantly better.

Neither approach is wrong — it depends on your brand and audience expectations.
