## The Quick Answer

These two VPNs barely compete on the same axis:

**Surfshark** is a mainstream consumer VPN — cheap, feature-packed, works on unlimited devices. Great for a family or a general-purpose privacy + geoblock tool.

**Mullvad** is a privacy-extremist tool — no accounts, no tracking, accepts cash payment. Fewer features, fewer servers, no streaming. Priced as a flat €5/mo with no deals.

If you\'re not specifically trying to sign up for a VPN without leaving any PII, Surfshark is almost certainly the better product for you. If you *are* trying to do that, Mullvad is one of two or three options that will let you (and the other is sketchier).

## Pricing

| Plan | Surfshark | Mullvad |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 1 month | $15.45 | €5 (~$5.49) |
| 1 year | $4.09/mo | **€5 flat** |
| 2 years | **$2.19/mo** (plus 2 months free) | €5 flat |
| Free plan | ✗ | ✗ |
| Money-back | 30 days | 30 days |

Mullvad\'s flat-pricing strategy is intentional — no discount bait. Over 2 years you pay $132 on Mullvad vs about $52 on Surfshark — roughly **2.5× more on Mullvad**. If you\'re price-sensitive, Surfshark wins hard.

**Edge: Surfshark** massively.

## Privacy & Anonymity

This is where Mullvad earns its premium:

**Mullvad:**
- **No email required** at signup — get a random 16-digit number.
- **Cash payment accepted** — mail an envelope of cash with your account number written on it. Genuinely anonymous.
- **Crypto accepted** — Monero, Bitcoin, Lightning.
- Based in **Sweden**. Independently audited by Cure53 (2020, 2022, 2023).
- **Open-source apps and server configurations**.
- **RAM-only servers** since 2023.
- **No user accounts or profiles at all.**

**Surfshark:**
- Email required. Account-linked.
- Card / PayPal / crypto accepted — anonymous only via crypto.
- Based in the **Netherlands** (14-Eyes adjacent, but Surfshark has argued this isn\'t a problem in their jurisdiction analysis).
- Independently audited by Deloitte and Cure53.
- RAM-only servers.
- Recent merger with Nord Security (2022) raised some eyebrows but they operate independently.

For "can this VPN be linked to me personally?" — Mullvad is decisively better. For technical privacy (audits, RAM-only, no-logs), they\'re comparable.

**Edge: Mullvad** on anonymity. **Tie** on technical privacy.

## Server network

- **Surfshark**: 3,200+ servers in 100 countries
- **Mullvad**: 700 servers in 38 countries

For travelers and anyone needing many different country exits, Surfshark\'s coverage is a real advantage.

**Edge: Surfshark** decisively.

## Speed

Both use WireGuard. On a 500 Mbps connection:

- **Surfshark**: 350-420 Mbps typical
- **Mullvad**: 350-450 Mbps typical

Essentially tied. Mullvad\'s smaller network means more variance depending on server load.

**Edge: Tie.**

## Streaming

- **Surfshark**: Reliably unblocks Netflix (multiple regions), Disney+, BBC iPlayer, Hulu, Amazon Prime Video. Has dedicated "Smart DNS" for consoles/TVs.
- **Mullvad**: Does NOT attempt to bypass streaming geo-blocks. You\'ll hit the Netflix proxy error regularly.

If streaming matters, Mullvad is out.

**Edge: Surfshark** decisively.

## Device limit

- **Surfshark**: Unlimited simultaneous connections. Genuinely unlimited — your whole household on one account.
- **Mullvad**: 5 devices.

For families, Surfshark\'s unlimited is a killer feature. 1 subscription = laptop + phone + iPad + Apple TV + smart TV + spouse\'s phone + router, etc.

**Edge: Surfshark** massively for multi-device users.

## When to choose which

**Choose Surfshark if you:**
- Want the cheapest mainstream VPN ($2.19/mo on 2-year)
- Need streaming to just work
- Have multiple people in a household to cover
- Want a single subscription covering VPN + antivirus + data-breach monitoring
- Are a regular privacy-conscious user (not a journalist, not a source)

**Choose Mullvad if you:**
- Consider anonymous-signup-with-cash a hard requirement
- Are a journalist, activist, or source who can\'t tie VPN to identity
- Don\'t care about streaming
- Trust the privacy-purist reputation more than feature checklists
- Are willing to pay ~2.5× for the anonymity guarantee

## Verdict

**For most users: Surfshark.** Price, features, streaming, unlimited devices — it\'s the better mainstream product.

**For journalists / activists / paranoid threat models: Mullvad.** Nothing else combines anonymous signup + cash payment + audited no-logs + open-source server config.

## FAQ

**Can I use Mullvad\'s anonymous signup to do illegal things?**
Mullvad doesn\'t help you avoid the authorities if you\'re doing something actually illegal. What anonymous signup *does* give you is deniability against mass surveillance, reputation protection against data breaches, and protection against correlation attacks. It\'s a privacy feature, not an impunity feature.

**Why is Surfshark so much cheaper?**
Volume. Surfshark targets the mainstream market with aggressive discounts and multi-year lock-ins. Mullvad prices for sustainability at its niche.

**Does Surfshark work in the UAE or China?**
Surfshark has specific "obfuscated" servers for restrictive-internet regions. It works most of the time in the UAE. In mainland China, reliability varies — ExpressVPN is still more reliable there.

**Is Surfshark\'s merger with Nord Security a privacy risk?**
The two companies operate independently post-merger. Each VPN maintains its own no-logs policy and audit track. The corporate relationship is primarily a back-office and commercial one, not a data-sharing one. That said, if you\'re privacy-extreme, Mullvad\'s fully independent ownership is a cleaner story.

**How do I pay Mullvad with cash?**
From their site: print your account number, put it in an envelope with cash (€5 per month you\'re paying for), and mail it to an address in Sweden. They credit your account when it arrives. Weird but it works.
