## The Quick Answer

**ActiveCampaign** if email automation is your primary need — lead scoring, complex workflows, CRM integration, and advanced segmentation. **GetResponse** if you want an all-in-one platform that includes webinars and sales funnels alongside email marketing.

## Pricing

| Contacts | ActiveCampaign (Starter) | GetResponse (Email Marketing) |
|----------|------------------------|------------------------------|
| 1,000 | $15/mo | $19/mo |
| 5,000 | $79/mo | $59/mo |
| 10,000 | $111/mo | $79/mo |
| 25,000 | $179/mo | $174/mo |

GetResponse is cheaper at 5K+ contacts. ActiveCampaign is cheaper at entry level. The pricing gap narrows at higher tiers.

## The Webinar Differentiator

GetResponse is the **only major email platform with built-in webinar hosting**. This means:

- No separate Zoom/WebinarJam subscription ($50-100+/month saved)
- Webinar registrants automatically added to your email list with tags
- Automated follow-up sequences triggered by webinar attendance
- Recording hosting included

If webinars are part of your marketing strategy (product demos, training, lead generation), GetResponse eliminates an entire tool from your stack.

## Automation Comparison

ActiveCampaign's automation builder is more powerful:

- **Conditional branching:** Full if/then/else logic vs GetResponse's simpler workflows
- **Lead scoring:** Built-in, customizable scoring models (GetResponse has this too, but it's less flexible)
- **CRM:** ActiveCampaign's built-in CRM with deal pipelines (GetResponse has basic CRM)
- **Predictive sending:** AI optimizes send time per individual contact

GetResponse's automation is good for most businesses. ActiveCampaign's is necessary for complex B2B sales cycles or sophisticated nurture sequences.

GetResponse can't match this level of conditional logic.</div>
