The Quick Answer

This is the closest VPN matchup in 2026. Surfshark wins on pure value — it’s $2.19/mo vs NordVPN’s $3.39/mo and offers unlimited device connections. NordVPN wins on raw performance — faster speeds, double the servers, and more advanced features.

If your budget is tight or you have a large family with many devices: Surfshark. If you want the fastest, most feature-rich VPN and don’t mind paying a bit more: NordVPN.

Surfshark’s monthly plan is actually more expensive than NordVPN’s. The value only kicks in on long-term plans. On the 2-year plan, Surfshark saves you roughly $29 over 2 years.

The bigger differentiator: Surfshark allows unlimited simultaneous connections. NordVPN caps at 10. If you have a family of 4, each with a phone, laptop, and tablet — that’s 12 devices. Surfshark covers all of them on one subscription.

Speed

NordVPN is faster, particularly on the NordLynx protocol:

  • NordVPN: 400-450 Mbps on nearby servers
  • Surfshark: 300-400 Mbps on nearby servers

The gap narrows on distant servers. Both handle 4K streaming and video calls without issues. You’ll only notice the speed difference on gigabit connections or when downloading large files.

Privacy

Both have independently audited no-logs policies. NordVPN’s VPN operations are registered in Panama (no data retention laws); its corporate parent Nord Security is headquartered in Lithuania. Surfshark is based in the Netherlands — EU member with robust GDPR protections.

Neither has had user logs or credentials exposed in any public incident. However, NordVPN did publicly confirm in 2019 that one of its Finnish datacenter servers had been accessed by an unauthorised third party in 2018 — no user data was compromised (none is kept), but the incident itself was real. Since then, NordVPN moved to a stricter colocated-server vetting model. Surfshark has no comparable public incident on record.

Ownership note: Nord Security merged with Surfshark in February 2022. Both brands continue to operate as independent products with separate infrastructure, apps, and teams — but they now share a corporate parent. Whether that matters is a judgement call; some users prefer unrelated ownership for competitive-privacy reasons.

FAQ

Are NordVPN and Surfshark owned by the same company?
Yes — Nord Security merged with Surfshark in February 2022, so both brands now share a corporate parent. They operate as independent products with separate infrastructure, apps, and teams. If ownership concentration matters to you (e.g. you want your VPN provider to be genuinely unrelated to competitors), a provider outside the Nord Security + Kape consolidation — like Mullvad (Amagicom AB, Sweden) — may be a better fit.
Has NordVPN ever had a data breach?
NordVPN publicly confirmed in 2019 that one of its Finnish datacenter servers was accessed by an unauthorised third party in 2018. No user logs, credentials, or traffic data were exposed (NordVPN keeps none). The company has since moved to a colocated-server model with stricter vetting. Surfshark has no comparable public incident on record.
Do the intro prices renew at the same rate?
No. Both NordVPN's $3.39/mo and Surfshark's $2.19/mo are 2-year introductory rates — renewals are materially higher. Before committing, check in-app what the renewal rate will be, or plan to rotate (cancel before renewal, resubscribe with a fresh intro deal).
Which is better for streaming in India?
Both work with Hotstar, Netflix India, and Amazon Prime Video India. NordVPN has more Indian server locations, which may provide faster connections if you're connecting from within India. Surfshark works fine for basic streaming but occasionally needs server switching.
Can I use Surfshark on my router?
Yes. Both NordVPN and Surfshark support manual router configuration (OpenVPN/WireGuard). This lets you protect all devices on your network without installing individual apps. With Surfshark's unlimited connections, a router setup covers your entire household.
