The Quick Answer

ActiveCampaign is for businesses that take email seriously — lead nurturing sequences, sales automation, CRM integration, and sophisticated segmentation. Mailchimp is for businesses that want something quick and simple to send newsletters and basic campaigns.

If you’ll outgrow “send a newsletter every week,” start with ActiveCampaign. You’ll save the migration headache later.

FAQ

I'm just starting out with 200 subscribers. Which should I pick?
Start with Mailchimp's free plan. At 200 subscribers, you don't need advanced automation yet. When you hit 1,000+ subscribers and want to build proper nurture sequences, evaluate ActiveCampaign. Migrating at that point is straightforward.
Which has better deliverability?
ActiveCampaign consistently ranks in the top tier for email deliverability. Their stricter account policies and smaller user base (compared to Mailchimp's 13M accounts) mean a cleaner sender reputation. Mailchimp's deliverability is good but has been more variable as they've scaled.
Can ActiveCampaign replace my CRM?
For small to mid-size businesses, yes. ActiveCampaign's built-in CRM handles contacts, deals, pipeline stages, and task management. It's not as deep as Salesforce or HubSpot, but for businesses under 50 employees, it eliminates the need for a separate CRM tool and the data sync headaches that come with it.
