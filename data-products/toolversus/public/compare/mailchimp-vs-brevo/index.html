The Quick Answer

Brevo if you have a large contact list and don’t need to email them every day. Mailchimp if you need its massive integration ecosystem or advanced e-commerce analytics.

The pricing model difference is the key: Mailchimp charges you for every contact on your list (even ones you haven’t emailed in months). Brevo charges you for emails actually sent. For most businesses, this means 40-60% lower costs with Brevo.

The Pricing Model That Changes Everything

Mailchimp: You pay based on how many contacts are in your list. 50,000 contacts = ~$385/mo, regardless of whether you email them once or 30 times.

Brevo: You pay based on emails sent per month. Unlimited contacts on every plan. 20,000 emails/month = $18/mo. Even at 150,000 emails/month, it’s only $65/mo.

Brevo’s Multi-Channel Advantage

Brevo isn’t just email — it’s a unified communication platform:

  • SMS marketing — send campaigns, transactional SMS
  • WhatsApp campaigns — huge for businesses in India, UAE, and MENA
  • Live chat — website chat widget included
  • Transactional email — SMTP relay for order confirmations, password resets
  • CRM — basic but functional contact management

Mailchimp focuses primarily on email marketing with some social media posting.

Where Mailchimp Wins

  • Integrations: 300+ vs Brevo’s ~150
  • E-commerce depth: Shopify revenue tracking, product recommendations, purchase behavior analytics
  • Email design: More templates, more polished drag-and-drop builder
  • Audience insights: Predictive demographics, lookalike audiences

FAQ

Is Brevo reliable for sending 100K+ emails per month?
Yes. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) serves 500,000+ businesses and handles billions of emails monthly. They're a major email infrastructure provider — many companies use their SMTP relay for transactional email. Deliverability is solid and on par with Mailchimp for most use cases.
Does Brevo work well for e-commerce?
For basic e-commerce email (abandoned cart, order confirmations, welcome series), Brevo is solid. For advanced e-commerce analytics (revenue attribution, product recommendations, purchase behavior segmentation), Mailchimp and Klaviyo are better. Brevo's strength is multi-channel — combining email + SMS + WhatsApp for e-commerce communications.
WhatsApp marketing — does this actually work?
In India and the Middle East, absolutely. WhatsApp has 95%+ open rates vs 20-25% for email. Brevo lets you send WhatsApp campaigns, automate WhatsApp messages (order updates, abandoned cart reminders), and manage conversations — all from the same platform as your email marketing. For businesses targeting India or UAE, this alone could justify choosing Brevo.
