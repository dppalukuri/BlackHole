The Quick Answer

Both are excellent platforms loved by creators and small businesses. MailerLite wins on pure value — more features, lower price, higher G2 rating (4.7 vs 4.4). ConvertKit (Kit) wins if you need built-in digital product sales, the Creator Network for audience growth, or you specifically identify as a content creator.

Email Design Philosophy

These tools take opposite approaches:

ConvertKit: Deliberately minimal, plain-text-focused emails. The philosophy is that simple emails feel more personal and have better deliverability. You can customize, but the defaults push you toward text.

MailerLite: Full drag-and-drop designer with rich templates. If you want visually polished newsletters with images, columns, and branded designs, MailerLite is significantly better.

Neither approach is wrong — it depends on your brand and audience expectations.

FAQ

I'm a YouTuber who sells courses. Which one?
ConvertKit. Its built-in commerce lets you sell courses and digital products directly to your subscribers without needing Teachable, Gumroad, or Shopify. Plus the Creator Network can grow your email list through recommendations from other creators in your niche.
I run a small business blog. Which one?
MailerLite. It includes a built-in blog, website builder, and landing pages — all at $10/mo. You get more features for less money, and the email designer is better for branded business communications. ConvertKit's creator-specific features (commerce, network) aren't relevant for a business blog.
Which has better free plan?
Depends on what you need. ConvertKit's free plan allows 10,000 subscribers but no automation or sequences — you can only send broadcasts. MailerLite's free plan allows 1,000 subscribers but includes automation, landing pages, and 12,000 emails/month. For most people, MailerLite's free plan is more functional.
