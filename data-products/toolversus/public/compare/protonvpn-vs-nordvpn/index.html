The Quick Answer

NordVPN is the better all-rounder. It’s cheaper ($3.39/mo vs $4.99/mo on 2-year plans), faster in almost every independent speed test, has more servers, and unblocks streaming services more reliably.

ProtonVPN wins if:

  • You already use Proton Mail / Drive (the Unlimited bundle is genuinely good value)
  • Open-source apps matter to you (ProtonVPN’s are fully OSS; NordVPN’s aren’t)
  • You want a free plan to test first
  • You’re in a heavily-surveilled situation where Swiss jurisdiction matters

Speed

Both use modern WireGuard-based protocols. On a 500 Mbps connection:

  • NordVPN (NordLynx): 400-450 Mbps typical on nearby servers
  • ProtonVPN (WireGuard): 380-440 Mbps typical on nearby servers

Within noise of each other for everyday use, but NordLynx consistently edges ProtonVPN in independent benchmarks (AV-TEST, PCMag).

Edge: NordVPN marginally.

Privacy architecture

This is where ProtonVPN earns its premium:

ProtonVPN:

  • Switzerland-based — outside 14-Eyes, strong privacy legislation.
  • Fully open-source apps on every platform — rare and genuinely significant.
  • Independently audited by SEC Consult (2022, 2024).
  • Secure Core multi-hop routes through hardened Swiss/Iceland/Sweden nodes.
  • Made by the team behind Proton Mail — recognised brand in the privacy-tool space.

NordVPN:

  • VPN operations registered in Panama (no data retention laws). Corporate parent Nord Security is headquartered in Lithuania; Panama jurisdiction is what governs user-data handling.
  • Apps are proprietary (not open source). The NordLynx protocol is a proprietary WireGuard-based implementation — unlike the standard WireGuard that ProtonVPN ships, it has not been independently protocol-audited.
  • Independently audited no-logs policy by Deloitte (2022, 2023) + PwC (2020).
  • Uses diskless / RAM-only servers (fully rolled out 2023).
  • Nord Security merged with Surfshark in 2022 — the two brands now share a corporate parent but operate separate infrastructure, apps, and teams.
  • In 2018, an unauthorised third party accessed one of NordVPN’s Finnish datacenter servers — no user logs or credentials were exposed (none are kept), and the incident was publicly confirmed by NordVPN in 2019. They’ve since moved to a colocated-server model with stricter vetting.

Both have strong audited no-logs records; ProtonVPN’s fully open-source stack is the more transparent of the two, and uses audited upstream WireGuard rather than a proprietary fork.

Edge: ProtonVPN on transparency and protocol openness; NordVPN on audit frequency.

Server network

  • NordVPN: 6,400+ servers in 111 countries
  • ProtonVPN: 5,800+ servers in 91 countries

For travelers, NordVPN’s larger country coverage matters — you’re less likely to end up on a distant server because yours doesn’t exist.

Edge: NordVPN.

Verdict

NordVPN for most mainstream users. The price gap, speed, server count, and streaming reliability are objective advantages. If you’re picking a VPN for a household, a family, or your main everyday privacy tool, it’s the better product.

ProtonVPN for privacy-forward users, existing Proton ecosystem customers, or anyone specifically wanting open-source apps. The free plan is also the best in the industry if you want to dip your toe in.

Both are trustworthy, both are audited, both are excellent. The choice is about fit, not fear.

FAQ

Is ProtonVPN’s free plan actually usable? Yes — genuinely. Unlimited data, 3 country options (US, NL, JP), one device at a time. Most “free VPN” services are either ad-supported or actively selling your data; ProtonVPN’s free plan is genuinely free and privacy-preserving because they treat it as a loss-leader for their paid product.

Can I use both at the same time? Not normally — VPN-over-VPN (“chained VPN”) usually breaks both providers’ kill switches and halves your speed. If you want multi-hop, use NordVPN’s Double VPN or ProtonVPN’s Secure Core instead of running two VPNs at once.

Which works better in the UAE / China? Both have obfuscated/stealth servers. NordVPN is slightly more reliable in the UAE based on independent reports. For mainland China, ExpressVPN is still the most reliable; neither NordVPN nor ProtonVPN are guaranteed to work.

Which is faster for 4K streaming? Both are fast enough. On a 100 Mbps connection, both will run 4K without issues. On a gigabit connection, NordVPN slightly edges ProtonVPN, but you’re unlikely to notice the difference.

Is ProtonVPN worth the extra money just for Switzerland? Not on its own. Swiss jurisdiction is a real advantage vs Panama’s (though both are strong), but it’s a small edge that matters only if you’re in a specific threat model. For 99% of users, the price + speed + server count wins out.
