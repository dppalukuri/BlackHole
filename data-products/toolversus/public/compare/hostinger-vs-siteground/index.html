Hostinger vs SiteGround (2026): Budget vs Premium — Is 4x the Price Worth It?

Hostinger renews at $7.99/mo. SiteGround renews at $17.99/mo. Both claim fastest WordPress hosting. We test speed, support, and features to find the real winner.

Hostinger for value, SiteGround for premium support

Both are excellent hosts. Hostinger costs less than half at renewal ($7.99 vs $17.99) and has comparable speeds. The Quick Answer

Hostinger for most people — comparable speed at less than half the renewal price. SiteGround if customer support is your top priority or you’re an agency managing multiple client sites.

Same introductory price, but SiteGround’s renewal is 125% more expensive and gives you 1 website with 10 GB storage, while Hostinger gives 100 websites with 100 GB. Over 3 years at renewal prices, you’d pay $360 more with SiteGround.

Speed

Both are fast, but for different reasons:

  • Hostinger: LiteSpeed servers + built-in caching + NVMe storage
  • SiteGround: Custom SuperCacher + NGINX + proprietary CDN

In benchmarks, they’re within ~0.2 seconds of each other. Both achieve sub-1.5 second load times for optimized WordPress sites. Speed should not be your deciding factor.

Support — Where SiteGround Earns Its Premium

SiteGround’s customer support is genuinely best-in-class:

  • Average response time under 10 minutes
  • Support agents can actually solve technical problems (not just follow scripts)
  • WordPress-specific expertise — they’ll debug your plugin conflicts

Hostinger’s chat support is good but takes longer and is less technical. If you’re non-technical and need hand-holding, SiteGround’s support is worth the premium.

FAQ

I'm building a portfolio/business site. Which one?
Hostinger. A portfolio or small business site doesn't need SiteGround's premium support. Hostinger's AI builder, 100 website allowance, and lower pricing make it the practical choice. Invest the savings ($120+/year) into marketing instead.
I'm an agency managing 20+ client sites. Which one?
SiteGround GrowBig or GoGeek. The staging environments, Git integration, client management tools, and premium support justify the cost when you're managing client expectations and deadlines. A hosting issue with a client site needs immediate expert help — that's SiteGround's strength.
Which is better for WooCommerce?
Both work well. SiteGround has deeper WooCommerce optimization and staging tools. Hostinger has LiteSpeed + LSCache which handles WooCommerce traffic spikes better. For stores under $50K/month revenue, Hostinger is fine. For larger stores where downtime costs real money, SiteGround's support is valuable insurance.
