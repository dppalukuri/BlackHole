The Quick Answer

These tools serve fundamentally different audiences. Mailchimp is a general-purpose marketing platform — email, ads, social, landing pages, e-commerce integrations. ConvertKit (now Kit) is laser-focused on creators — writers, podcasters, YouTubers — and lets you sell digital products and subscriptions without needing Shopify or Gumroad.

If you’re a creator monetizing an audience: ConvertKit. If you’re running an e-commerce store or traditional business: Mailchimp.

ConvertKit’s free plan is radically more generous — 10,000 subscribers for free vs Mailchimp’s 500. But ConvertKit’s free tier lacks automation and sequences, which limits its usefulness.

At scale (10K+ subscribers), pricing converges. The difference is what you get for that money.

The Creator Economy Advantage

ConvertKit was built by Nathan Barry, a creator who needed a tool that Mailchimp didn’t offer. The differences show:

  • Sell digital products directly: ConvertKit has built-in commerce — sell ebooks, courses, memberships, paid newsletters without Shopify, Gumroad, or Teachable
  • Creator Network: Get recommended by other creators in the network — organic subscriber growth that Mailchimp doesn’t offer
  • Newsletter referral system: Built-in “refer a friend” for subscriber growth
  • Sponsorship marketplace: Sell ad spots in your newsletter directly

Mailchimp has none of these creator-specific features.

Where Mailchimp Wins

  • Integrations: 300+ vs ConvertKit’s ~120. If you need to connect with Shopify, WooCommerce, Salesforce, or niche tools, Mailchimp almost certainly has a native integration
  • Email design: Mailchimp’s drag-and-drop editor produces more visually polished emails. ConvertKit intentionally favors plain-text style (better deliverability, but less pretty)
  • Analytics: Mailchimp’s reporting is deeper — revenue attribution, audience insights, campaign performance comparisons
  • Multi-channel: Mailchimp does social posting, Google/Facebook ads, and postcards. ConvertKit is email-only

FAQ

Can I migrate from Mailchimp to ConvertKit?
Yes. ConvertKit offers free concierge migration for accounts with 5,000+ subscribers. For smaller lists, you can export a CSV from Mailchimp and import it into ConvertKit in minutes. Tags and segments don't transfer automatically — you'll need to rebuild them.
Which has better email deliverability?
ConvertKit generally has higher deliverability rates. Their plain-text-style emails avoid spam filters better than Mailchimp's heavily designed templates. ConvertKit also has stricter list hygiene requirements, which keeps their sender reputation cleaner.
I'm a blogger who also sells physical products — which should I use?
If physical products are a significant part of your revenue, Mailchimp's Shopify/WooCommerce integrations are stronger. If you primarily sell digital products (ebooks, courses, memberships) with occasional physical merch, ConvertKit handles it better with its built-in commerce.
