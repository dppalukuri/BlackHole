Speed

NordVPN is meaningfully faster. On a 500 Mbps connection, independent tests in 2024-2025 show:

  • NordVPN (NordLynx): 400-450 Mbps typical
  • CyberGhost (WireGuard): 300-380 Mbps typical

CyberGhost uses standard WireGuard while NordVPN’s NordLynx is a WireGuard-based but optimised implementation. For everyday use (video calls, browsing, 4K streaming) either is fast enough. For gigabit connections or latency-sensitive tasks (gaming, video editing), NordVPN is the better tool.

Edge: NordVPN.

Privacy & audits

Both have no-logs policies. Audit frequency and rigor differ:

NordVPN:

  • Audited by Deloitte (2022, 2023) and PwC (2020).
  • VPN operations registered in Panama (no data retention laws); corporate parent Nord Security is headquartered in Lithuania. The Panama jurisdiction is what governs user-data handling.
  • RAM-only server infrastructure (fully rolled out 2023).
  • In 2018, one of NordVPN’s Finnish datacenter servers was accessed by an unauthorised third party — no user logs or credentials were exposed (none are kept), but the incident was real and publicly confirmed by NordVPN in 2019. They’ve since moved to a colocated-server model with stricter vetting.
  • In 2022, Nord Security merged with Surfshark — both brands now share a corporate parent but operate separate infrastructure, apps, and teams.

CyberGhost:

  • Audited by Deloitte (2024) — their first major no-logs audit.
  • Based in Romania — EU member, but outside 14-Eyes direct participation.
  • Maintains “NoSpy” servers in their Romanian HQ as an extra layer.
  • Owned by Kape Technologies, which also owns ExpressVPN and Private Internet Access — consolidation concerns for privacy purists (Kape was previously called “Crossrider” and had adware history in 2013-2016).

Kape’s ownership of multiple major VPNs (CyberGhost + ExpressVPN + PIA under one parent) is a real concern for some users. Nord Security’s merger with Surfshark raises a similar, if smaller, question — both now share a parent company. If ownership concentration matters to you, Mullvad (independently owned by Amagicom AB, Sweden) is the cleanest story in the category.

Edge: NordVPN on audit frequency; both have ownership caveats worth knowing about.

Server network

  • CyberGhost: 11,700+ servers in 100 countries
  • NordVPN: 6,400+ servers in 111 countries

CyberGhost has nearly 2x the server count but covers fewer countries. For travelers, NordVPN’s broader coverage is more useful than CyberGhost’s denser coverage.

Edge: Depends on use case. CyberGhost for torrenting (more servers = more load distribution); NordVPN for traveling.

Verdict

NordVPN wins. Price premium is real but worth it for the speed, audit record, streaming consistency, and the Nord Security vs Kape ownership difference.

CyberGhost is a legitimate budget choice. Don’t mistake it for a scam — it’s a real product with a real audit (2024), real RAM-only infrastructure, and genuinely cheap multi-year pricing. Just go in knowing you’re trading speed + consistency for the dollars saved.

Most people should pick NordVPN. If you specifically can’t pay more than ~$50/year for a VPN, CyberGhost is the right choice within that constraint.

FAQ

Is CyberGhost safe to use? Yes. Despite Kape’s adware past (as Crossrider in 2013-2016), CyberGhost’s modern infrastructure is legitimate — independently audited by Deloitte (2024), uses RAM-only servers, keeps no logs. The “Kape owns both ExpressVPN and CyberGhost” concern is a corporate-structure one, not a technical-safety one.

Why is CyberGhost so much cheaper? Different market strategy. CyberGhost targets mass-market budget-shoppers with aggressive 2-year discounts. NordVPN positions slightly higher and invests more in speed + audits. Both are sustainable businesses.

Does CyberGhost work in China? Inconsistently. You can connect sometimes, but it’s not reliable enough to depend on for work. NordVPN’s obfuscated servers are more consistent, and ExpressVPN is still the gold standard for China reliability.

Does NordVPN offer a free trial? No direct free trial, but a 30-day money-back guarantee that’s genuinely honoured. Install, use for up to 30 days, request a refund — confirmed no-hassle in multiple independent reviews. CyberGhost offers 45 days.

Can I stack CyberGhost + NordVPN for more privacy? Not recommended — VPN-over-VPN typically breaks both kill switches and halves speed. Use NordVPN’s built-in Double VPN instead if you want multi-hop.
